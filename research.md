  <h1>Research</h1>
  <div class="prose research-flow paragraph-tight-indent">
    <p>My dissertation uses the life and writings of Mizancı Mehmed Murad Bey to examine how Ottoman political opposition, public intellectual life, and modern historical consciousness were made across imperial borderlands. Rather than treating Murad only as a Young Turk or an Ottoman exile, the project follows his movement from Dagestan and the Russian imperial educational system to Istanbul, Paris, Geneva, and Cairo. It argues that his political thought took shape through the negotiation of several distinct worlds: Caucasian Muslim society, Russian imperial schooling, Ottoman bureaucratic and intellectual circles, and the transnational press. Through this trajectory, the dissertation shows how late Ottoman intellectual life was not simply produced at the imperial center, but through the unstable movement between periphery and center, loyalty and opposition, biography and worldmaking, offering a new way of understanding how opposition and belonging were thought together in the late Ottoman world.</p>

    <p>My research examines the making of Ottoman intellectual authority, public life, historical writing, journalism, and political judgment from the late Ottoman Empire to modern Turkey and the broader Middle East. The Research section is divided into three parts.</p>
