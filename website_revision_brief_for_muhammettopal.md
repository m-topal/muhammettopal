# Revision Brief for muhammettopal.com / GitHub Pages Site

This file explains the changes needed for the current GitHub Pages site. The goal is to move the site from a functional skeleton to a polished, minimalist, charismatic academic personal website with a distinct Blog section.

## 1. Overall Concept

The site should feel like a refined academic personal page with a literary, archival, newspaper-like atmosphere. It should not look like a generic academic template, a tech portfolio, or a university department page.

Desired feeling:

- Minimal but charismatic
- Warm paper background
- Elegant serif typography
- Deep burgundy accent color
- Archival, editorial, scholarly atmosphere
- Serious but personal
- Text-first, but visually attractive
- No fake Ottoman decoration, no heavy motifs, no corporate look

The homepage should function like a billboard: short, attractive, and immediately clear.

The Blog should function as a separate living track: essays, notes, videos, podcasts, and fragments, organized by categories, themes, formats, dates, tags, and series.

## 2. Navigation Structure

Keep the main navigation:

- Home
- Research
- Teaching
- Publications
- CV
- Contact

But treat Blog differently. It should not look like just one more normal tab. It should have a special place at the far right, visually separated from the stable academic pages.

Example:

Home | Research | Teaching | Publications | CV | Contact        [Blog]

The Blog button can be an outlined pill, a small bordered button, or a subtly highlighted link. On the Blog page, the Blog button can appear filled or slightly darker to show that the visitor is inside the Blog section.

Important: Do not underline Blog in the top nav in a way that makes the homepage feel like the Blog tab is active.

## 3. Homepage Revision

The homepage should not repeat all the content from Research, Teaching, Publications, CV, and Contact. Those already have their own tabs.

The homepage should be a simple attractive case, almost a billboard.

It should answer:

1. Who is Muhammet Topal?
2. What does he work on?
3. What is this website?
4. Where should people go next?

Suggested homepage structure:

### Header / Hero

Large name:

Muhammet Topal

Short identity line:

Historian of the late Ottoman Empire, intellectual life, political opposition, print culture, and the Caucasus.

Short paragraph:

This website brings together my research, teaching, publications, CV, and contact information. The Blog is a separate, living notebook for essays, notes, videos, podcasts, and fragments, an ongoing record of ideas in progress.

Current status:

PhD candidate in History and Middle Eastern & North African Studies, University of Arizona.

Visual:

Use one strong archival image or a refined visual anchor. The image should be atmospheric but not overwhelming. Prefer archival Istanbul, newspaper, manuscript, archive table, or another historical visual. Avoid a generic photo placeholder.

### Remove from homepage

Remove large repeated cards for Research, Teaching, Publications, CV, and Contact if the top navigation already includes these pages. If some guidance is needed, use a very small line of text links, not large cards.

### Blog teaser

At the bottom of the homepage, include a small section:

From the Blog

Show three recent or featured items:

- one essay
- one podcast
- one video

Each item should be compact, with title, type, date, and one-line description.

This section should show that the site is alive, but it should not dominate the homepage.

## 4. Blog Page Revision

The Blog page should keep the same warm archival aesthetic as the homepage. It should not become a completely different modern magazine website.

Replace any previous title such as Defter with:

Blog

Subtitle:

Essays, notes, videos, podcasts, and fragments.

Alternative longer subtitle:

Essays, notes, videos, podcasts, and fragments, a living notebook on Ottoman history, intellectual life, and public writing.

### Blog page layout

At the top:

- Blog title
- short subtitle
- optional archival image related to writing, newspapers, manuscripts, notebooks, or printing

Then add a search bar near the top:

Search essays, notes, podcasts, videos...

Around or below the search bar, add a toolbar:

Themes | Formats | Date | Tags | Series

Each toolbar item can later open a dropdown or link to an archive page.

### Blog content sections

Do not make the Blog page only a chronological scroll with pagination.

Instead, organize it as an editorial archive:

1. Featured Essays
2. Featured Podcasts
3. Featured Videos
4. Latest Notes & Fragments
5. Browse by Theme
6. Browse by Format
7. Browse by Date

Possible themes:

- Ottoman Empire
- Turkey
- Caucasus
- Archives
- Print Culture
- Intellectual Life
- Public Life
- Books
- Personal Notes

Possible formats:

- Essays
- Notes
- Videos
- Podcasts
- Translations
- Archive Finds
- Fragments

### Blog post cards

Each item should show:

- title
- date
- format, such as Essay, Note, Podcast, Video
- one short description
- theme or tag
- image/audio/video indicator if applicable

For videos, use embedded YouTube or Vimeo later.

For podcasts, use embedded Spotify, SoundCloud, or another podcast host later.

Do not host large audio or video files directly in the GitHub repository.

Decide whether the CV page is:

Option A: Minimal page with only a download button and short summary.

or

Option B: Full web CV with sections.

Avoid the awkward middle version.

Recommended for now:

- Short summary
- Download CV button
- Selected highlights only

Example:

For a complete list of education, appointments, publications, teaching, grants, and service, please download my full CV.

[Download CV]

## 11. Design Details

Use:

- Warm off-white paper background
- Near-black main text
- Deep burgundy accent
- Soft gray-brown secondary text
- Elegant serif font for headings
- Readable serif or humanist sans-serif for body text
- Generous white space
- Thin borders, not heavy boxes
- Soft archival image treatment

Avoid:

- Bright blue links
- Generic Bootstrap/card-heavy template feeling
- Heavy icons
- Too many boxes on homepage
- Decorative Ottoman patterns
- Fake parchment overload
- Modern tech-blog look
